Tab 1 Feed
• Purpose: A logging dashboard used to track, feed, and audit the ongoing health of your sourdough starter.
• Feed Amount Inputs: Captures exact input weights in grams for flour, water, and starter inoculations to monitor daily feeding ratios.
• Enriched Dough Toggle: Features a specialized sugar switch to log data variations when feeding sweet doughs or specialized cultures.
• Flour Blend Sliders: Uses a smooth slider interface to precisely record custom percentage ratios of All-Purpose (AP) versus Whole Wheat (WW) flours.
• Initial Metric Logs: Records physical baseline numbers, including initial acidity (pH) and starter volume (in milliliters), right after a fresh mix.
• Visual Baseline: Includes an integrated camera icon to instantly attach a "Just Fed" photo, giving you a visual height benchmark to measure against the peak rise.
• Start Feed Timer: this starts a timer on the screen showing elapsed time since the most recent refresh.

Feed: Active Feed
• Purpose: The live tracking interface used to monitor starter fermentation variables in real time during an active refresh cycle. 
• Active Elapsed Timer: Features a prominent digital stopwatch at the top coupled with a status subtitle to measure exact fermentation duration. 
• Dynamic Ratio Summary: Displays active feeding weights, automatically calculating the true baker's ratio and exact flour composition matrix. 
• Real-Time Ambient Metrics: Includes a sequential log timeline tracking elapsed minutes, pH drop intervals, and dough temperatures via a dedicated "+ Log Reading" button. 
• Inline Progression Curve: Integrates a micro-graph visualization directly into the feed tab, mapping current data entries against your long-term baseline. 
• Session Termination Target: Features a prominent "Mark as Peak" action button to lock in the final fermentation time and save session parameters. 

Tab 2: Graph
• Purpose: A data viz screen that plots your starter's historical acidity curve over time to verify fermentation speeds.
• pH Over Time Graph: Traces real-time data points against elapsed hours, visualizing how quickly your culture drops its pH levels.
• Data Resolution Axis: Tracks timelines across a tight horizontal window (which scales as time exceeds the window) matched against vertical acidity metrics spanning from 5.8 down to 3.2.
• Visual Indicators: Maps your current feed points with bright red dots, contrasting them against a solid black line that represents your historical "Vitality Average."
• Timeframe Filtering: Includes an "All-Time" button toggle to show the all-time average (which is off by default to keep the interface clean).

Tab 3: Recipe
• Purpose: Serves as your master formula library and creation hub.
• Empty State: Displays a centralized book icon and a call-to-action to build a recipe when no formulas exist.
• Recipe Identity Cards: Summarizes saved formulas into compact cards showing the title, creation date, and total phase count.
• Linear Pipeline Tags: Visualizes your chosen recipe workflow as a chronological chain of horizontal phase tags for quick verification.
• Data Portability: Features an integrated export and share control button to easily back up or distribute recipe files.

Recipe: Recipe Builder
• Purpose: Initial setup screen for naming and launching a blank baking template.
• Title Input: Provides an open text field to name your custom formula before building its timeline.
• Add Phase Redirect: Features a prominent structural button to open the workflow taxonomy selection menu.

Recipe: Recipe Builder: Phase Selection
• Purpose: A comprehensive, categorized taxonomy list used to choose your specific baking steps.
• Pre-Processing Category: Features structural setup options like Building the Levain, Scalding, Toasting the Flour, and Soaking Seeds/Grains.
• Mixing Category: Provides precise physical choices, contrasting standard Autolysing with Fermentolysing, alongside advanced techniques like Bassinage and Delayed Salt.
• Fermentation & Shaping Categories: Offers structural manipulation milestones including Stretching and Folding, Laminating, Preshaping, Bench Resting, and Stitching.
• Proofing & Baking Categories: Concludes your workflow choices with options like Cold Retarding, Proofing, Scoring, and Baking.

Recipe: Recipe Runner
• Purpose: The launchpad dashboard for preparing and initiating a live bake session.
• Dynamic Checklist: Pulls your master recipe phases into a vertical list with checkboxes.
• Flexible Skipping: Lets you uncheck specific phases to dynamically skip them on today's live bake without changing your master template.
• Clean Condensed Rows: Automatically collapses rows with blank ingredient fields to keep your startup screen clear of clutter.

Recipe: Recipe Runner: Active Session
• Purpose: The active dashboard used while you are hands-on in the kitchen during a live bake.
• Dynamic Scaling Controls: Provides instant global multiplier buttons (0.5x to 3x) that flash a reminder banner to help you scale your ingredient math.
• Non-Linear Navigation: Features independent, active "Start" buttons next to every step row, allowing you to jump around or execute steps out of order based on how your dough looks.
• Independent Step Timers: Tracks the real-time duration of your active step alongside a global phase counter and a segmented progress bar.
• Phase specs: Per phase sections that act as read-only instructions from the Recipe Builder for that instruction.

Recipe: Recipe Runner: Active Session: Journal Overlay
• Purpose: A full-screen scratchpad used to log reflections on how your active baking session went.
• Global Logging Canvas: Provides an open text ledger at the top to record variables unique to today's bake.
• Tokenized Phase Chips: Allows you to tag comments or reflections to the phase in which you were working when you made the note.

Tab 4: Calendar
• Purpose: Your high-level monthly log and baking schedule journal.
• Activity Metric Badges: Highlights three key statistics at the top: "This Month" (total entries), "Day Streak" (consecutive log history), and "Total Feeds" (lifetime data sum).
• Current Date Anchor: Automatically flags the current date with a circular badge to show your place in the month.
• Data History Feed: Features a bottom activity ledger that populates with historical notes and logs when you select specific calendar days.
• Cloud Synchronization: Displays an automated cloud icon and time stamp verifying successful profile data backups.

Tab 5: About
• Purpose: Provides app information and customer support access.
• Logo: Official branding for Waterview Bakehouse, the maker of this app.
• Developer Contact: Email the developer to submit bugs, feature requests, or project feedback.
